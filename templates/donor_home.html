About Blood Donation

A blood bank is a facility or organization that collects, tests, processes, and stores blood and blood products for distribution to hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare facilitiesThe primary purpose of a blood bank is to ensure a safe and adequate supply of blood for medical treatments, surgeries, and emergencies..

Blood banks organize and conduct blood donation drives to collect blood from voluntary donors. The collected blood is then transported to the blood bank for further processing.

Upon receiving blood donations, the blood bank performs various tests to determine blood type (A, B, AB, or O) and Rh factor (positive or negative). Additionally, tests are conducted to screen for infectious diseases such as HIV, hepatitis, and syphilis.
